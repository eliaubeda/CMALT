Contextual statement

Backgorund, Skills and Experience

In January 2022 I joined King’s Online (King’s College London) as an E-Learning Content Developer. This was a career shift for me, with the hope to combine two of my greatest passions: education and technology. Both of these started to take shape back in 2015, when I had my first experience teaching in HE and I became interested in web design and coding.

Teaching

I have worked in Higher Education since 2015, teaching at the Universitat de Barcelona and The University of York, doing course administration at The London Institute of Banking & Finance and now working at King’s College London.

I have been a guest lecturer multiple times at the Universitat de Barcelona’s department of History of Art, where I also designed and delivered a Level 5 module in 2021. At The University of York, I worked as a Graduate Teaching Assistant (GTA) from 2017 to 2021 at the departments of Sociology, History of Art, and the Centre for Women’s Studies. During that time I became increasingly interested in pedagogy and became an Associate Fellow of AdvanceHE after doing the York Learning and Teaching Award (2017-2018), a credit-baring taught course on pedagogic practice for which I received the Outstanding Portfolio Award.

Technology

In 2015, during my MA in Women’s Studies, I discovered web design and coding as a means to convey an experience. I taught myself to code HTML and CSS to develop my dissertation as a pseudo-hypertext; my aim was to put the reader in an active role where they had to choose their own path and, in a way, mimic the experience of engaging in the creative practices I was analysing.

My interest in coding and web design grew and in 2018 I was invited to re-design the website for the Centre for Women’s Studies’ annual conference, which is still being to date, and the following year I took a course in Web Development by Code First Girls where I developed my CSS and Java skills. This was very useful as that same year I was curating an art exhibition as part of my PhD research and I was able to design and build my own website.

Combining the two

In 2021 I reconsidered my career path and decided to shift my course to pursue my passions for pedagogy and technology through a role in the world of Learning Technology. At that point I joined King’s Online and started my journey in E-learning and learning technology.

Why I wish to gain CMALT accreditation and how?

Achieving my CMALT accreditation is another step in this direction, and a big part of my professional development. Writing this portfolio has helped me review the work I have done in the past few years and reflect on how I want to move forward in the world of learning technology.

... and how this relates to my future career aspirations.

Being part of the ALT community will allow me to network and share ideas on what is an ever-changing field. I want to continue growing and enhancing my skills and knowledge by attending courses, conferences and workshops. Not only that, but I want to start contributing to those events and be an active part of the learning technology community.

Aurèlia Puigdomènech | 2023
