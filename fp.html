Future plans

My passion for pedagogy and technology brought me to the world of e-Learning, and they continue to inform my professional development.

Pedagogy

Reflection

One of the most rewarding aspects of my job is problem-solving design queries where I apply my knowledge of the technologies we use, pedagogical approach and awareness of accessibility concerns. It can range from re-designing an activity with an unnecessary table to make it more understandable and accessible, to finding new creative ways of using tools available to us to encourage active and collaborative learning. I also enjoy and learn a lot from the Professional Development workshops I deliver around accessibility and the accessible use of our technological tools; designing the training and teaching sessions allows me to cement my knowledge and consider how it is relevant to my colleagues and our work.

Action

Therefore, I plan to continue developing my training and teaching skills as well as my knowledge of pedagogy. I want to provide better learning sessions to my colleagues and open them to the wider King’s community, and contribute to the design of our courses with more pedagogically informed and research-based suggestions.

Technological horizon scanning

Reflection

Another area where my two passions meet is in the evaluation of technological tools. I revel in the challenge of assessing potential new tools for our department, testing them for their pedagogical value and accessibility. This allows me to apply my understanding of our pedagogical approach and education needs and my knowledge of accessibility standards, universal design and our business model in terms of scalability and sustainability.

Action

I want to grow in this area and start proactively searching for new technological solutions to our education needs and goals. In this regard, there are two key areas I need to develop:

  • Accessibility: the more I learn about accessibility the more I know I need to keep learning. I want to keep my knowledge up to date with the latest accessibility standards and best practices in the rapidly changing e-Learning landscape, particularly with the use of AI tools.

  • Moodle: although I work with and build content in Moodle daily, I want to learn about the platform from various perspectives, so I can have a more holistic understanding of it. Amongst other things, I am interested in learning about the reporting capabilities of Moodle, how integration with other tools such as SITS, plugins and Turnitin work, or how Moodle is used for administrative purposes in the faculties.

Aurèlia Puigdomènech | 2023
